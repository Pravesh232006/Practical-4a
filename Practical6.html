Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Known as the "Missile Man of India", he played a critical role in India's defense and space programs. Kalam was deeply committed to transforming India into a developed nation through science, technology, and education.

Major Achievements:

  • Led India's missile development programs (AGNI, PRITHVI).
  • Key architect of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Directed SLV-III project that launched India’s first satellite.
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007).
  • Formulated Vision 2020 for transforming India into a developed nation.
  • Launched PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas).
  • Wrote inspirational books: Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, India 2020, etc.
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, and Padma Bhushan.
  • Established the Kalam-Raju stent to help cardiac patients affordably.
  • Inspired millions of youth through speeches, books, and student interactions.
  • Continued teaching and inspiring until his last breath, delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Timeline:

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and led SLV-III project
  • 1980: India’s first satellite launched under his leadership
  • 1998: Played a leading role in nuclear tests at Pokhran
  • 2002: Became President of India
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
